Curriculum Vitae

I recently completed my bachelor's degree in computer science. Now, I am specializing in disease discovery and progression. I am a research collaborator at MLC and HuggingFace Science. My work centers on under-researched reproductive disease, where I apply machine learning and statistical methods to uncover biological insights.

Alongside my computational work, my interest in connecting data-driven discoveries with experimental validation lead me to collaborate with the CMHS research group at ABUAD to translate computationally inferred targets into early, non-invasive diagnostics approaches. I will be sharing my vision and journey on YouTube.

Beyond research, I enjoy exploring creative projects such as woodworking and innovative footwear design. I find that hands-on work helps me think through problems differently and it often inform how I approach technical and scientific challenges.

About

My work is grounded in developing computational methods that engage with biological complexity. I am training in computational biology, focusing on ML and statistical models for disease discovery under real-world constraints such as sparse, heterogeneous data. I am interested in interpretable, causally informed models for biological hypothesis generation.

I value teaching and community engagement and currently work as a Coding and Robotics Instructor for primary and secondary school students at EduPoint Ltd..
