Curriculum Vitae

I recently completed my bachelor's degree in computer science. Now, I am specializing in disease discovery and progression. I am a research collaborator at MLC and Hugging Science, working with Steven. My work centers on under-researched reproductive disease, where I apply machine learning and statistical methods to uncover biological insights.

Alongside my computational work, I am interested in connecting data-drive discoveries with experimental validation. I am also collaborating with the CMHS research group at ABUAD to translate computationally inferred targets into early, non-invasive diagnostics approaches. I will be sharing my vision and journey on YouTube.

Beyond research, I enjoy exploring creative projects such as woodworking and innovative footwear design. I find that hands-on work helps me think through problems from differently and it often inform how I approach technical and scientific challenges.

About

My work is grounded in the belief that meaningful biomedical discovery requires computational methods that engage with biological complexity. I am training toward research in computational biology, focusing on ML and statistical models for disease discovery and progression under real-world constraints such as sparse and heterogeneous data. I am interested in interpretable, causally informed models that generate hypotheses for biological validation.

Beyond research, I value teaching and hands-on work, which continue to shape how I think about problem solving and scientific inquiry.
